Brunei took some measures and ban travelers coming from Hubei Province

Home / Uncategorized / Brunei took some measures and ban travelers coming from Hubei Province
Scott Klein   Uncategorized   0   February 4, 2020

Health Minister YB Dato Seri Setia Dr. Hj Mohammad Isham Hj Jaafar said that anyone who has visited the province of Hubei, where coronavirus epidemic happened– will be refused entry to Brunei in the past 14 days, except for Brunei citizens and permanent resident.

The minister cautioned in a press conference to discuss the precautionary measures that the possibility of cases of coronavirus transmitted into Brunei is “considered high” even though no cases have been identified in the country so far. YB Dato Dr. Hj Mohd Isham said that anyone who has been in China during the past two weeks will also have to experience 14 days of self-isolation.

In China, the virus killed 170 people and infected 7,711. Coronavirus cases have been reported in at least 21 countries throughout Asia, the Middle East, Europe, and North America. The Ministry of Education stated that since December 2019 42 out of 73 Brunei students have been evacuated from China.

The health minister said another 29 students are expected to return to Brunei on Friday. All of the students will undergo self-isolation for 14 days.

“The Ministry of Foreign Affairs is working on bringing [home] the remaining two students in the near future,” he added.

Thirty-nine employees from Hengyi Industries who recently arrived from China to Brunei for the celebration of the Lunar New Year are also in self-isolation over 14 days. Students and Hengyi workers will be moved for further examination to the Tutong Isolation Center if they show symptoms, the Minister of Health said.

Brunei citizens and residents were also urged not to fly to the Hubei province and to cancel unnecessary travel to China.

All of these steps are temporary and from time to time will be checked, he added. Brunei has also canceled official visits to China, and at this time, government officials are instructed not to fly to China.

After the growing number of confirmed cases and reports of human-to-human transmission in other countries, Brunei started thermal screening at his international airport since January 21st.

Additionally, incoming passengers will be required to submit a health declaration form while incoming passengers in cruise ships and other vessels are also subject to temperature screening.

“Businesses will feel the pinch of the impact of the virus and we just have to accept that. Our side [Brunei], as well as China, are trying our best to stop the spread of this virus”.
